Prompt

> *Two trains are moving toward each other on parallel tracks. Train A 
> travels at 60 km/h, and Train B at 90 km/h. The initial distance between 
> them is 150 km. How long (in seconds) will it take for the trains to 
> meet?*  
>   
> **Requirements:**  
> 1. Convert all speeds to **m/s** (meters per second) using:  
>    `1 km/h = 1000/3600 m/s = 5/18 m/s`.  
> 2. Calculate the **relative speed** (since they move toward each other). 
>  
> 3. Use the formula: `Time = Distance / Relative Speed`.  
> 4. Convert the final answer to **seconds** (not hours or minutes).  
> 5. Show every calculation step (no shortcuts).  
>   
> **Output Format:**  
> ```  
> Step 1: Convert Train A speed to m/s:  
> [Your calculation]  
> Step 2: Convert Train B speed to m/s:  
> [Your calculation]  
> Step 3: Relative speed = [A] + [B] = [result] m/s  
> Step 4: Time (seconds) = [Distance in meters] / [Relative speed]  
> Step 5: Final answer: [Number] seconds  
> ```
